  • Why study about Vietnamese and Balinese cultures?

    To learn more about Asia and for personal growth.

    To extend your learning in practical dealings with peoples from Asia, in business and all occupations.

    Vietnam and Bali (Indonesia) are two dynamic emerging economies, both offering a host of opportunities and challenges. They are representative of countries having received cultural influences from China and India.

    Rich historical backgrounds, exceptional landscapes and fascinating cultures are trademarks of both countries.

    Vietnam’s history is complex and captivating. Archaeological findings support that Vietnamese civilization has existed for over half a million years, making Vietnam amongst one of the oldest ancient cultures in the world. This country has a long history of war and political conflict due to foreign domination. Since 1973, Vietnam has entered a period of peace, stability and growth so much so that tourism is now one of Vietnam’s top priorities.

    Bali is an Indonesian island, located between Java and Lombok. Approximately 93% of its population adheres to a form of Hinduism, called Balinese Hinduism. Due to the strength of Bali’s thriving culture, it has survived foreign domination and has been able to maintain its traditions intact. It is one of the largest tourist destinations in South East Asia due to its artistic nature, harmonious ambiance and the islands stunning beauty.
