Xin Chao Vietnam
(switch to french version)
Travel indeed forms one’s youth. This year, Maurice-Lavallée organized the farthest trip in the history of our school, both culturally and geographically. In spring of last school year, from March 20th to April 4th we visited Vietnam. Living for two weeks in an Asian country certainly required a great level of adaptation but also served to open our minds and allowed us to gain knowledge about what it means to be a citizen of the world.
After many hours of training, our group of 30 students from the 11th and 12th grade, 2 teachers and 2 administrators, and over 15 hours in transit, we arrived in Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam. During the two following weeks we had a very busy schedule. Factors such as the 13 hour time difference, a hot and humid climate, different customs and a completely foreign language lead us to discover a new way of life and taught us to respect what is different from us.

Vietnam is a very open, warm and welcoming communist country in the process of development. We went to Halong Bay in Northern Vietnam, which is globally recognized by UNESCO as a natural World Heritage site. We were also fortunate enough to live unforgettable experiences during our time in the village of Maï Chau. The beauty of the rice fields and majestic mountains of the Red River Delta amazed us. By visiting Ho Chi Minh, also known as Saigon, the most populous city in Vietnam, we observed the influence of Americans, Chinese, and French and many other cultures that at one time or another dominated this small country. The Mekong Delta helped us to understand the importance of rice for the country’s economy and in the daily life of Vietnamese people. Our group was dazzled by the food and especially by the amount of rice served in each of our meals. Our trip to southern Vietnam allowed us to experience a new historical perspective of the imperialist era and the Vietnamese War (known to the Vietnamese as the “American War”).

Thousands of images and memories will remain etched in our minds for a long time and it is thanks to our middle school, our parents, the team from Ben Thanh Tourist Travel and Ms. Claudette Croteau, the coordinator of our journey, that we will for a long time, dream of the burning sun and beautiful beaches of the China Sea.

Dominique Goudreau and Stéphane Erickson
